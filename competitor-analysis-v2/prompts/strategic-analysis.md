# Strategic Analysis Prompt (Phase 1)

You are analyzing competitor ad copy to assess strategic market positioning.

---

## Task

Score the provided ad using 15 strategic attributes (0-3 points each, 45 max total).

For each attribute:
1. Extract relevant evidence from the ad copy
2. Score based on criteria below
3. Justify your score with specific quotes

---

## The 15 Strategic Attributes

### Category 1: Credibility Signals (9 points max)

#### 1. Authority Claims (0-3 points)
**Look for:** Rankings, awards, certifications, "official" status

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Specific, quantified claim (#1, rated X/5, specific award name)
- **2 points:** Generic superlative (#1, best, leading)
- **1 point:** Implied authority (trusted by, used by)
- **0 points:** No authority claim

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "Rated 4.9/5 By Shop Owners"
- 2 pts: "#1 Auto Repair Software"
- 1 pt: "Trusted by professionals"
- 0 pts: No claim

---

#### 2. Social Proof (0-3 points)
**Look for:** Customer counts, testimonials, usage stats

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Specific numbers (10,000+ customers, X businesses)
- **2 points:** Implied scale (thousands, widely used)
- **1 point:** Generic social proof (trusted, recommended)
- **0 points:** No social proof

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "10,000+ Shops Use Tekmetric"
- 2 pts: "Used by thousands"
- 1 pt: "Trusted by shop owners"
- 0 pts: No proof

---

#### 3. Trust/Reliability Signals (0-3 points)
**Look for:** Guarantees, uptime claims, support promises

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Explicit guarantee (works every time, 99.9% uptime, money back)
- **2 points:** Reliability claim (reliable, dependable, proven)
- **1 point:** Implied trust (professional, enterprise-grade)
- **0 points:** No trust signal

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "Works Every Single Time"
- 2 pts: "Reliable shop management"
- 1 pt: "Professional-grade software"
- 0 pts: No signal

---

### Category 2: Value Proposition (9 points max)

#### 4. Primary Benefit/Promise (0-3 points)
**Look for:** Main outcome promised (save time, make money, reduce hassle)

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Specific outcome (save X hours, increase Y%)
- **2 points:** Clear benefit category (save time, increase revenue, reduce costs)
- **1 point:** Generic improvement (better, easier, faster)
- **0 points:** No benefit stated

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "Save 10 Hours Per Week"
- 2 pts: "More Time. Less Paperwork"
- 1 pt: "Better shop management"
- 0 pts: Only features, no benefits

---

#### 5. Solution Category/Positioning (0-3 points)
**Look for:** How they categorize themselves

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Clear differentiated category (all-in-one, specialized, platform)
- **2 points:** Generic category (software, solution, system)
- **1 point:** Implied category (through features only)
- **0 points:** No category stated

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "All-In-One Auto Repair Shop Software"
- 2 pts: "Auto Shop Management Software"
- 1 pt: Features imply it's software
- 0 pts: No category

---

#### 6. Unique Mechanism/Differentiator (0-3 points)
**Look for:** What makes THIS solution different/special

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Specific unique feature/approach
- **2 points:** Comparative claim (more than, easier than, faster than)
- **1 point:** Generic differentiator (innovative, modern, advanced)
- **0 points:** No differentiator

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "Revolutionary Video-Based DVI"
- 2 pts: "More Features, Easier To Use"
- 1 pt: "Modern shop software"
- 0 pts: No differentiator

---

### Category 3: Target Audience (6 points max)

#### 7. Audience Specificity (0-3 points)
**Look for:** Who is this FOR?

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Very specific segment (independent shops, multi-location franchises)
- **2 points:** Broad but defined (shop owners, retailers, businesses)
- **1 point:** Generic (professionals, teams, users)
- **0 points:** No audience specified

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "Built For Independent Auto Shops"
- 2 pts: "For Shop Owners"
- 1 pt: "For professionals"
- 0 pts: No audience

---

#### 8. Use Case/Application (0-3 points)
**Look for:** What scenario/job is this solving?

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Specific use case scenario
- **2 points:** General application (manage, track, organize)
- **1 point:** Implied use case (through features)
- **0 points:** No use case

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "Manage Multi-Location Auto Shop Chains"
- 2 pts: "Manage Invoicing & Scheduling"
- 1 pt: Features imply management use
- 0 pts: No use case

---

### Category 4: Features/Capabilities (9 points max)

#### 9. Feature Mentions (Count) (0-3 points)
**Look for:** How many specific features are named?

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** 5+ features listed
- **2 points:** 3-4 features listed
- **1 point:** 1-2 features listed
- **0 points:** No specific features

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "DVI, Estimates, Inventory, Job Progress, Invoices, Scheduling, Reports" (7 features)
- 2 pts: "Invoicing, Scheduling & DVI" (3 features)
- 1 pt: "Digital inspections" (1 feature)
- 0 pts: No features named

---

#### 10. Technical/Integration Claims (0-3 points)
**Look for:** Tech stack mentions, integrations, platforms

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Specific tech/platform (cloud-based, mobile app, API, integrations)
- **2 points:** Generic tech claim (modern, digital, online)
- **1 point:** Implied technology
- **0 points:** No tech mention

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "Cloud-Based With Mobile App & 100+ Integrations"
- 2 pts: "Modern digital platform"
- 1 pt: "Online software" (implied tech)
- 0 pts: No tech mention

---

#### 11. Operational Benefits (0-3 points)
**Look for:** How it changes operations/workflow

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Specific operational improvement
- **2 points:** General workflow benefit
- **1 point:** Implied operational change
- **0 points:** No operational benefit

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "Unlimited Users - No Per-Seat Fees"
- 2 pts: "Streamlines operations"
- 1 pt: Features imply operational change
- 0 pts: No operational benefit

---

### Category 5: CTA & Urgency (6 points max)

#### 12. CTA Type (0-3 points)
**Look for:** What action are they asking for?

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Specific, low-friction CTA (demo, free trial, see pricing)
- **2 points:** Generic CTA (learn more, get started, contact us)
- **1 point:** Implied CTA (click to learn)
- **0 points:** No CTA

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "Get a Free Demo" / "Start Free Trial"
- 2 pts: "Learn More" / "Get Started"
- 1 pt: Implicit click
- 0 pts: No CTA

---

#### 13. Urgency/Scarcity (0-3 points)
**Look for:** Time-bound offers, limited availability, "now" language

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Explicit urgency (limited time, today only, X spots left)
- **2 points:** Soft urgency (now, today, don't wait)
- **1 point:** Implied urgency (fast, quick, immediate)
- **0 points:** No urgency

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "Limited Time: 50% Off First Month"
- 2 pts: "Start Your Trial Now"
- 1 pt: "Fast setup"
- 0 pts: No urgency

---

### Category 6: Emotional Hooks (6 points max)

#### 14. Pain Point Addressed (0-3 points)
**Look for:** What problem/frustration does this solve?

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Explicit pain point (no more X, stop Y, eliminate Z)
- **2 points:** Implied pain (save time = currently wasting time)
- **1 point:** Generic improvement (better than current)
- **0 points:** No pain addressed

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "No More Paperwork Hassles"
- 2 pts: "Save Time" (implies time waste)
- 1 pt: "Better than spreadsheets"
- 0 pts: No pain mentioned

---

#### 15. Aspiration/Transformation (0-3 points)
**Look for:** What positive future state is promised?

**Scoring:**
- **3 points:** Specific transformation (become X, achieve Y, transform to Z)
- **2 points:** Generic aspiration (better business, success, growth)
- **1 point:** Implied improvement
- **0 points:** No aspiration stated

**Examples:**
- 3 pts: "Transform Your Repair Process"
- 2 pts: "Grow your business"
- 1 pt: "Better results" (implied)
- 0 pts: No aspiration

## Important Rules

1. **Only score what's explicitly in the ad copy.** Don't assume or infer beyond what's clearly stated.

2. **Use exact quotes as evidence.** Pull specific phrases from the ad.

3. **Be strict with scoring.** If in doubt between two scores, choose the lower one. Better to underscore than overscore.

4. **Justify every score.** Explain why you assigned that particular score vs. higher or lower.

5. **Count features literally.** For attribute 9, count each distinct feature mentioned (e.g., "Invoicing, Scheduling, DVI" = 3 features).

6. **Consider keyword context.** If analyzing for a specific search term, note how well the ad matches that term.

7. **Identify archetype.** Based on which categories score highest, determine the ad's strategic approach.
