# RSA Distribution Formulas

Strategic composition formulas for creating balanced, high-performing RSA ad sets.

---

## Overview

Google Ads RSAs require:
- **15 headlines** (minimum 3, maximum 15)
- **4 descriptions** (minimum 2, maximum 4)

For optimal performance, use the **full 15 headlines + 4 descriptions** with strategic distribution across different types.

## Detailed Headline Breakdown

### 1. Keyword-Focused Headlines (3)

**Purpose**: Ensure relevance to search query, improve Quality Score, match search intent

**Framework Elements**:
- #1: Keyword in Headline
- #2: Intent vs Specificity (funnel matching)

**Patterns**:
1. **Direct Keyword**: "{Keyword}" or "{Keyword} {Location}"
   - "Emergency Plumber Austin"
   - "Oil Change Near Me"
   - "Brake Repair Service"

2. **Keyword + Descriptor**: "{Adjective} {Keyword} {Descriptor}"
   - "Expert Emergency Plumber"
   - "Affordable Oil Change Service"
   - "Professional Brake Repair"

3. **Keyword + CTA**: "{Keyword} - {CTA}"
   - "Emergency Plumber - Call Now!"
   - "Oil Change - Book Today!"
   - "Brake Repair - Get Free Quote!"

**Requirements**:
- ✅ Must include ad group keyword (or close variant)
- ✅ ≤30 characters
- ✅ Title Case
- ✅ Match funnel stage (top: educational, middle: comparison, bottom: urgent)

---

### 2. Social Proof Headlines (2)

**Purpose**: Build trust, reduce perceived risk, demonstrate authority

**Framework Elements**:
- #18: Social Proof
- #21: Positive Sentiment

**Patterns**:
1. **Rating + Review Count**: "Rated {X}★ By {Y}+ Customers"
   - "Rated 4.9★ By 2000+ Customers"
   - "Rated 5.0★ By 500+ Happy Drivers"

2. **Review Snippet**: "\"{Quote}\" - {Source}"
   - "\"Best Shop in Austin\" - Customer"
   - "\"Honest & Reliable\" - Google Review"

3. **Credential + Social Proof**: "{Credential} + {Years/Count}"
   - "BBB A+ Rated Since 2010"
   - "AAA Approved - Trusted By 5000+"

4. **Years + Track Record**: "{Years} of {Positive Outcome}"
   - "30+ Years of Excellent Service"
   - "Trusted Since 1985"

**Requirements**:
- ✅ Must be verified (from website or GMB reviews)
- ✅ ≤30 characters
- ✅ Title Case
- ✅ Positive sentiment (0.8-1.0)

**If No Reviews Available**:
- Use credentials instead: "BBB A+ Rated", "ASE Certified Since 2005"
- OR skip social proof headlines and create additional flexible headlines

---

### 3. Generic USP Headlines (4)

**Purpose**: Differentiate from competitors, highlight features+benefits

**Framework Elements**:
- #15: Features and Benefits
- #16: Single Focus

**Patterns**:
1. **Credential + Descriptor**: "{Credential} {Descriptor}"
   - "Licensed & Insured Mechanics"
   - "ASE Certified Technicians"
   - "Factory-Trained Specialists"

2. **Experience + Proof**: "{Years} of {Proof/Outcome}"
   - "30+ Years of Experience"
   - "Family Owned Since 1985"
   - "Over 5000 Cars Serviced"

3. **Convenience Feature**: "{Convenience Benefit}"
   - "Same-Day Appointments Available"
   - "Open 6 Days A Week"
   - "Free Loaner Cars Available"

4. **Pricing/Value Proposition**: "{Value Statement}"
   - "Competitive Pricing Guaranteed"
   - "Free Estimates - No Surprises"
   - "Flexible Payment Plans Available"

**Requirements**:
- ✅ Must be verified from website
- ✅ One focus per headline (don't list everything)
- ✅ ≤30 characters
- ✅ Title Case

---

### 4. CTA Headlines (2)

**Purpose**: Drive action, increase conversion rate

**Framework Elements**:
- #4: Call to Action
- #5: Imperative Verb
- #23: Continuity (must appear on landing page)

**Strong Imperative Verbs**: Call, Book, Get, Schedule, Order, Start, Try, Shop, Request, Download

**Patterns**:
1. **CTA + Benefit + Urgency**: "{CTA} For {Benefit} {Timeframe}!"
   - "Call Now For Same-Day Service!"
   - "Book Today & Save 10%!"
   - "Schedule Your Free Inspection!"

2. **CTA + Value Prop**: "{CTA} {Value Proposition}!"
   - "Get Your Free Quote Today!"
   - "Request A Free Estimate!"
   - "Call For Competitive Pricing!"

**Requirements**:
- ✅ Starts with imperative verb
- ✅ Includes punctuation (! or ?)
- ✅ ≤30 characters
- ✅ CTA must appear on landing page (continuity)
- ✅ Title Case

---

### 5. Pun Headline (1)

**Purpose**: Memorable, shows brand personality, stands out

**Framework Elements**:
- #22: Puns (very good OR purposely bad)

**Rules**:
- Must be industry-relevant
- Must be on-brand
- Either very clever OR intentionally cheesy (no middle ground)
- Use sparingly (1 per RSA set maximum)

**Auto Repair Examples**:
- "Brake For Quality Service"
- "We'll Steer You Right"
- "Don't Let Problems Exhaust You"
- "We've Got You Covered - Bumper To Bumper"
- "We're Driven To Excellence"

**Plumbing Examples**:
- "Don't Let Problems Drain You"
- "We're Flush With Solutions"
- "Your Pipe Dream Come True"

**Requirements**:
- ✅ Industry-specific wordplay
- ✅ ≤30 characters
- ✅ Title Case
- ✅ On-brand tone

**If You Can't Write a Good Pun**:
- Skip it and create an additional flexible headline instead
- Bad puns are worse than no puns

---

### 6. Flexible Headlines (3)

**Purpose**: Fill gaps, test new concepts, address specific needs

**Framework Elements**: Mix of optional elements based on business/audience

**Suggested Mix**:
1. **FOMO/Urgency** (#14): "Limited Time: 20% Off Service!"
2. **Proximity** (#10): "Serving Austin & Surrounding Areas"
3. **Problem/Solution** (#17): "Car Won't Start? We Can Help!"

**Other Options**:
- **Hours/Availability**: "Open Evenings & Weekends"
- **Service Area**: "Mobile Service - We Come To You"
- **Specialty**: "European Vehicle Specialists"
- **Guarantee**: "100% Satisfaction Guaranteed"
- **Award/Recognition**: "Best of Austin 2024"

**Requirements**:
- ✅ Fill strategic gaps in headline set
- ✅ ≤30 characters
- ✅ Title Case
- ✅ Verified from website (if specific claim)

## Description Breakdown

### Type 1: Keyword + Product USP + CTA (2 descriptions)

**Purpose**: Conversion-focused, specific, action-oriented

**Formula**: "{Keyword} {Specific Feature/USP}. {Secondary USP}. {CTA}!"

**Pattern**:
```
[Keyword] By [Specific Credential/USP]. [Generic USP]. [CTA]!
```

**Examples**:
- "Oil Change By ASE Certified Technicians. Licensed & Insured. Call Today For Same-Day Service!"
- "Expert Brake Repair With Lifetime Warranty. Free Estimates Available. Book Your Appointment Now!"
- "Emergency Plumbing By Licensed Master Plumber. 24/7 Service Available. Call For Immediate Help!"

**Requirements**:
- ✅ Starts with keyword or keyword phrase
- ✅ Includes at least 1 specific USP (verified from website)
- ✅ Ends with CTA (imperative verb)
- ✅ ≤90 characters
- ✅ Title Capital Case
- ✅ Punctuation for emphasis

**Character Budget**:
- Keyword phrase: 15-25 chars
- Specific USP: 20-30 chars
- Generic USP: 15-20 chars
- CTA: 10-20 chars
- Total: ~80-90 chars

---

### Type 2: Keyword + Generic USPs (no CTA) (2 descriptions)

**Purpose**: Trust-building, informational, credibility

**Formula**: "{Keyword} {Generic USP 1}. {Generic USP 2}. {Generic USP 3}."

**Pattern**:
```
[Keyword] By [Business Type]. [Credential]. [Experience/Location].
```

**Examples**:
- "Oil Change By Family-Owned Auto Shop. Serving Austin Since 1985. ASE Certified Mechanics."
- "Brake Repair By Licensed & Insured Technicians. Over 30 Years Experience. AAA Approved Shop."
- "Plumbing Service By Trusted Local Experts. BBB A+ Rated. Available 24/7 For Emergencies."

**Requirements**:
- ✅ Starts with keyword or keyword phrase
- ✅ Includes 2-3 generic USPs (verified from website)
- ✅ No CTA (informational tone)
- ✅ ≤90 characters
- ✅ Title Capital Case
- ✅ Builds credibility

**Character Budget**:
- Keyword phrase: 15-25 chars
- Generic USP 1: 15-25 chars
- Generic USP 2: 15-25 chars
- Generic USP 3: 15-25 chars
- Total: ~80-90 chars

## Technical Requirements (All Headlines & Descriptions)

### Character Limits (Hard Limits)
- Headlines: **30 characters maximum** (including spaces)
- Descriptions: **90 characters maximum** (including spaces)

### Capitalization
- **Title Case**: Capitalize First Letter Of Each Word
- Exceptions: a, an, the, and, but, or, for, nor, on, at, to, from, by, in (unless first or last word)

### Punctuation & Symbols
- **Encouraged**: ! ? $ % & - / : ; '
- Use for emphasis and stand-out
- CTAs should end with !
- Questions should end with ?

### Sentiment
- Target: **0.8-1.0** (positive sentiment)
- Avoid: <0.6 (too neutral or negative)

### Verification
- All USPs must be verified from website
- All reviews must be from website or GMB (SERP API)
- Never assume features/services not found on website

## Generation Workflow

1. **Gather Inputs**:
   - Primary keyword (ad group name)
   - Business name
   - Business overview
   - Generic USPs (4+) from website
   - Specific USPs (4+) from website
   - Reviews (2-5) from website or GMB

2. **Generate Headlines**:
   - 3 keyword-focused (using primary keyword)
   - 2 social proof (from reviews/rating)
   - 4 generic USPs (from website USPs)
   - 2 CTAs (imperative verbs + urgency)
   - 1 pun (industry-relevant)
   - 3 flexible (fill gaps)

3. **Generate Descriptions**:
   - 2 with keyword + product USP + CTA
   - 2 with keyword + generic USPs (no CTA)

4. **Validate**:
   - All headlines ≤30 chars
   - All descriptions ≤90 chars
   - Title Case applied
   - All claims verified from website
   - CTAs appear on landing page

5. **Output**:
   - Headline text | Character count | Type | Sentiment score
   - Description text | Character count | Type | Sentiment score

## Validation Checklist

Before finalizing RSA set:

**Headline Checklist**:
- ✅ 15 headlines total
- ✅ All ≤30 characters
- ✅ 3 contain primary keyword
- ✅ 2 have social proof (or credentials if no reviews)
- ✅ 4 showcase generic USPs
- ✅ 2 have clear CTAs
- ✅ 1 has pun (or skip if not good enough)
- ✅ 3 flexible headlines fill gaps
- ✅ All Title Case
- ✅ All verified from website

**Description Checklist**:
- ✅ 4 descriptions total
- ✅ All ≤90 characters
- ✅ 2 have keyword + product USP + CTA
- ✅ 2 have keyword + generic USPs (no CTA)
- ✅ All Title Case
- ✅ All verified from website
- ✅ CTAs match landing page

**Continuity Checklist**:
- ✅ All CTAs appear on landing page
- ✅ All specific claims (prices, guarantees, hours) visible on page
- ✅ All credentials/certifications mentioned can be verified on page
