# Tactical Scan Prompt (Phase 2)

You are analyzing competitor ad copy to assess conversion effectiveness and execution quality.

---

## Task

Evaluate the provided ad using 7 tactical attributes (conversion optimization focus).

For each attribute, flag as:
- ✅ **Present (3 points):** Strong execution, clear and effective
- ⚠️ **Weak (2 points):** Present but poorly executed or unclear
- ❌ **Missing (0 points):** Absent or undetectable

Maximum score: 21 points (7 attributes × 3 points)

---

## The 7 Tactical Attributes

### 16. Search Term Relevance (0-3 points)
**Evaluate:** Does the ad match the user's search intent? Is the keyword visible in headline or description?

**Why it matters:** Google rewards relevance with higher Quality Score and lower CPC. Users skip irrelevant ads.

**Evaluation criteria:**
- ✅ **Present (3 pts):** Search term appears in headline AND description, natural usage
- ⚠️ **Weak (2 pts):** Search term only in description OR awkward keyword stuffing
- ❌ **Missing (0 pts):** Search term not found in ad copy

**Analysis checklist:**
- [ ] Is the keyword in the headline?
- [ ] Is the keyword in the description?
- [ ] Does it feel natural or forced?
- [ ] Would a user recognize this ad matches their search?

**Examples:**
- Search: "auto shop management software"
- ✅ Title: "Auto Shop Management Software" + Description: "Cloud Based Shop Management"
- ⚠️ Title: "Revolutionary Shop Software" + Description: "for auto shops"
- ❌ Title: "Shopmonkey" + Description: generic features, no keyword

---

### 17. CTA Quality (0-3 points)
**Evaluate:** Is the call-to-action specific, action-driven, and clear? Does it tell the user exactly what to do next?

**Why it matters:** Vague CTAs reduce conversion rates. "Get Started" converts worse than "Start Free Trial."

**Evaluation criteria:**
- ✅ **Present (3 pts):** Specific action verb + clear outcome (Start Free Trial, Get a Demo, See Pricing)
- ⚠️ **Weak (2 pts):** Generic CTA (Learn More, Get Started, Click Here)
- ❌ **Missing (0 pts):** No explicit CTA in ad copy

**Analysis checklist:**
- [ ] Is there an explicit CTA in the ad?
- [ ] Does it use a strong action verb?
- [ ] Is the outcome clear? (what happens after clicking)
- [ ] Is it specific vs. generic?

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Get a Demo of Our Revolutionary Shop Software"
- ✅ "Start Your Free 30-Day Trial Now"
- ⚠️ "Visit our site to learn more"
- ⚠️ "Get Started"
- ❌ Just listing features with no action requested

**Note:** This evaluates execution quality. Strategic Attribute 12 (CTA Type) evaluates conversion strategy.

---

### 18. Features vs Benefits Distinction (0-3 points)
**Evaluate:** Does the ad clearly separate WHAT it does (features) from WHY it matters (benefits)? Or does it only list features?

**Why it matters:** Benefits sell, features inform. "Save 10 hours per week" (benefit) > "Automated scheduling" (feature).

**Evaluation criteria:**
- ✅ **Present (3 pts):** Clear benefits stated separately from features (outcome-focused language)
- ⚠️ **Weak (2 pts):** Benefits implied but not explicit (features only, user must infer value)
- ❌ **Missing (0 pts):** Only features listed, no benefits communicated

**Analysis checklist:**
- [ ] Are benefits explicitly stated? (save time, increase revenue, reduce hassle)
- [ ] Are benefits separate from feature lists?
- [ ] Does the ad explain WHY features matter?
- [ ] Is there outcome-focused language?

**Benefit language:** Save, increase, reduce, eliminate, gain, achieve, improve, transform
**Feature language:** Has, includes, features, comes with, offers

**Examples:**
- ✅ "More Time. Less Paperwork" (benefit) + "Digital Vehicle Inspections, Invoices" (features)
- ✅ "Save 10 Hours Per Week With Automated Scheduling & Invoicing"
- ⚠️ "Cloud Based Shop Management, Unlimited Users" (features only, benefit implied)
- ❌ "DVI, Estimates, Inventory, Invoices" (just a feature list)

---

### 19. Promotional Hooks (0-3 points)
**Evaluate:** Are there discounts, bonuses, special offers, or time-bound incentives?

**Why it matters:** Promotional hooks increase CTR and urgency. "Save 20%" outperforms no offer.

**Evaluation criteria:**
- ✅ **Present (3 pts):** Specific offer (X% off, free trial, bonus, limited-time deal)
- ⚠️ **Weak (2 pts):** Generic incentive (special pricing, contact for offer)
- ❌ **Missing (0 pts):** No promotional element

**Analysis checklist:**
- [ ] Is there a discount? (%, $X off)
- [ ] Is there a free trial? (with specific duration)
- [ ] Are there bonuses? (free setup, free training)
- [ ] Is there a limited-time offer?

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Start Your Free 30-Day Trial Now"
- ✅ "Save 20% - Limited Time Offer"
- ✅ "Free Setup & Training Included"
- ⚠️ "Special pricing available"
- ⚠️ "Contact us for an offer"
- ❌ Standard offering with no incentive

---

### 20. Specificity & Numbers (0-3 points)
**Evaluate:** Does the ad use concrete numbers, data points, percentages, or prices instead of vague claims?

**Why it matters:** Specificity builds credibility and makes claims tangible. "10,000+ shops" > "widely used."

**Evaluation criteria:**
- ✅ **Present (3 pts):** Multiple specific numbers (customer count, percentage, price, metric)
- ⚠️ **Weak (2 pts):** One number mentioned, or vague quantification (thousands, many)
- ❌ **Missing (0 pts):** No numbers, all generic claims

**Analysis checklist:**
- [ ] Are there customer counts? (10,000+, 5,000 businesses)
- [ ] Are there ratings/scores? (4.9/5, 98% satisfaction)
- [ ] Are there percentages? (Save 30%, 2x faster)
- [ ] Are there prices? ($49/month, free for 30 days)
- [ ] Are there metrics? (10 hours saved, 50% more revenue)

**Count ALL numbers in the ad**

**Examples:**
- ✅ "10,000+ Shops" + "Rated 4.9/5" + "Save 10 Hours/Week" (3 numbers)
- ⚠️ "Thousands of customers" (vague quantification)
- ⚠️ "Used by 5,000+ businesses" (1 number)
- ❌ "Widely used by shop owners" (no numbers)

---

### 21. Location/Audience Callouts (0-3 points)
**Evaluate:** Does the ad explicitly mention the target location, industry, or audience segment?

**Why it matters:** Personalization increases relevance. "For Austin Auto Shops" > generic "For Auto Shops."

**Evaluation criteria:**
- ✅ **Present (3 pts):** Specific geographic or demographic targeting (Austin, independent shops, multi-location owners)
- ⚠️ **Weak (2 pts):** Broad targeting (auto shops, shop owners, retailers)
- ❌ **Missing (0 pts):** No audience or location mentioned

**Analysis checklist:**
- [ ] Is there a location mention? (city, state, region)
- [ ] Is there industry specificity? (auto shops, plumbing, HVAC)
- [ ] Is there segment targeting? (independent, franchise, multi-location)
- [ ] Is there size targeting? (small business, enterprise)

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Built For Independent Shops in Texas"
- ✅ "For Multi-Location Auto Repair Franchises"
- ⚠️ "Made For Auto Shops"
- ⚠️ "For Shop Owners"
- ❌ Generic "Shop Management Software"

---

### 22. Social Proof Execution (0-3 points)
**Evaluate:** HOW effectively is social proof presented? Is it quantified, credible, and specific?

**Why it matters:** "Rated 4.9/5 by 10,000+ shops" converts better than "trusted by shops."

**Evaluation criteria:**
- ✅ **Present (3 pts):** Quantified proof with external validation (ratings, reviews, specific customer counts, awards)
- ⚠️ **Weak (2 pts):** Generic proof (trusted, recommended, used by shops)
- ❌ **Missing (0 pts):** No social proof

**Analysis checklist:**
- [ ] Is there a rating/review score? (4.9/5, 5 stars)
- [ ] Is there a specific customer count? (10,000+, 5,000 businesses)
- [ ] Is there external validation? (G2 rating, Capterra award, Better Business Bureau)
- [ ] Is there a testimonial quote?

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Rated 4.9/5 By 10,000+ Shop Owners"
- ✅ "Winner: Best Auto Shop Software 2024"
- ⚠️ "Trusted by shop owners nationwide"
- ⚠️ "Used by thousands"
- ❌ No social proof mentioned

**Note:** This evaluates execution quality. Strategic Attribute 2 (Social Proof) evaluates presence/strength.

## Important Rules

1. **Be strict with flags.** Don't give ✅ unless execution is truly strong. When in doubt, use ⚠️.

2. **Use exact quotes as evidence.** Pull specific phrases that justify your flag.

3. **Consider keyword context.** For Attribute 16, you MUST know the search keyword to evaluate relevance.

4. **Count all numbers.** For Attribute 20, literally count every number in the ad (customer counts, ratings, percentages, prices, metrics).

5. **Distinguish features from benefits.** For Attribute 18:
   - Features = what it has/does
   - Benefits = why it matters/outcome

6. **Evaluate execution, not just presence.** An ad can have social proof (✅ present in strategic layer) but execute it poorly (⚠️ weak in tactical layer).

7. **Flag quick wins.** Identify attributes flagged ❌ that could easily become ✅ with minor changes.
