# Ad Copy Examples

Complete examples demonstrating good vs bad ad copy, plus the "Four Words to Value" rule for website headlines.

---

## The "Four Words to Value" Rule

**Principle**: Website headlines must deliver clear value within the first 4 words to create cognitive momentum (pull reader forward).

### Rule Components

1. **Start with Verb** - Action-oriented, creates movement
2. **Subject/Predicate Structure** - "You [Verb] [Benefit]"
3. **Use Most Important Keyword** - Prioritize customer's interest
4. **Avoid "We"** - Make customer the subject, not your business
5. **Imply Value, Not Work** - Emphasize how easy it is

### Formula Pattern

```
[Verb] [Your] [Benefit/Need] [Action/Timeframe] [Supporting Detail]
  ↑      ↑        ↑              ↑                     ↑
 Word 1 Word 2  Word 3         Word 4           Rest of headline
```

### Good Examples

✅ **"Get Emergency Plumbing Fixed Today"**
   - Word 1-4: "Get Emergency Plumbing Fixed"
   - Verb: Get (action)
   - Customer benefit: Emergency plumbing fixed
   - Timeframe: Today
   - Value clear: Your emergency will be resolved

✅ **"Fix Your Leaky Pipes Fast"**
   - Word 1-4: "Fix Your Leaky Pipes"
   - Verb: Fix (action)
   - Customer focus: Your pipes
   - Speed: Fast
   - Value clear: Problem solved quickly

✅ **"Save 20% On Oil Changes"**
   - Word 1-4: "Save 20% On Oil"
   - Verb: Save (benefit)
   - Specific value: 20% discount
   - Service: Oil Changes
   - Value clear: Money saved

✅ **"Book Same-Day Brake Repair Now"**
   - Word 1-4: "Book Same-Day Brake Repair"
   - Verb: Book (action)
   - Speed: Same-Day
   - Service: Brake Repair
   - Value clear: Immediate availability

### Bad Examples

❌ **"We Provide Expert Plumbing Services"**
   - Starts with "We" (business-focused, not customer-focused)
   - No clear value in first 4 words
   - Generic, vague
   - No action or benefit

❌ **"Our Team Of Certified Mechanics Is Here To Help"**
   - Too long before value is mentioned
   - Passive structure ("is here to help")
   - Focus on business, not customer need
   - Value buried at end

❌ **"Welcome To Example Auto Repair"**
   - First 4 words: "Welcome To Example Auto"
   - No benefit, just business name
   - No action or value proposition
   - Wasted headline space

❌ **"Quality Auto Repair Services Since 1985"**
   - Vague "quality" claim
   - No specific benefit
   - No action verb
   - History ≠ value to customer

### Transformation Examples

**Before → After**:

1. "We Have Been Serving Austin For 30 Years"
   → "Get Trusted Service - 30+ Years Experience"

2. "Our ASE Certified Technicians Are Ready To Help You"
   → "Fix Your Car Problems - ASE Certified Experts"

3. "Welcome To Our Family-Owned Auto Repair Shop"
   → "Trust Family-Owned Quality Since 1985"

4. "Let Us Take Care Of All Your Plumbing Needs"
   → "Solve Your Plumbing Problems Fast"

## Good vs Bad Headline Comparisons

### Keyword-Focused Headlines

| Bad ❌ | Why Bad | Good ✅ | Why Good |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| "plumbing service available" | Lowercase, vague, no location | "Emergency Plumber Austin TX" | Title Case, specific service, location |
| "BEST AUTO REPAIR SHOP!!!" | All caps, excessive punctuation, unverifiable | "Expert Auto Repair - ASE Certified" | Proper case, verified credential |
| "Click here for oil change" | Generic CTA, no value | "Affordable Oil Change Near Me" | Benefit (affordable), proximity |

### Social Proof Headlines

| Bad ❌ | Why Bad | Good ✅ | Why Good |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| "Many happy customers" | Vague, no numbers | "Rated 4.9★ By 2000+ Customers" | Specific rating and count |
| "Best shop in town!!" | Unverifiable, excessive punctuation | "BBB A+ Rated Since 2010" | Verifiable credential with timeframe |
| "5 star reviews" | Lowercase, no count | "Rated 5.0★ On Google - 500+" | Title Case, platform specified, count |

### Generic USP Headlines

| Bad ❌ | Why Bad | Good ✅ | Why Good |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| "we are licensed and insured" | Lowercase, "we" focus | "Licensed & Insured Mechanics" | Title Case, customer-focused, & saves chars |
| "Quality service" | Vague, no proof | "ASE Certified Technicians" | Specific, verifiable credential |
| "Experienced mechanics available for hire" | Too long (42 chars) | "30+ Years Of Experience" | Concise, specific, uses + symbol |

### CTA Headlines

| Bad ❌ | Why Bad | Good ✅ | Why Good |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| "Contact us" | Vague, weak | "Call Now For Same-Day Service!" | Specific action, benefit, urgency |
| "click here to learn more" | Lowercase, generic, no value | "Get Your Free Quote Today!" | Title Case, benefit (free), urgency |
| "give us a call today please" | Lowercase, too polite, no urgency | "Book Your Appointment Now!" | Title Case, imperative, immediate |

### Pun Headlines

| Bad ❌ | Why Bad | Good ✅ | Why Good |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| "We're tired of bad service" | Confusing, vague pun, "we" focus | "Brake For Quality Service" | Clear auto pun, customer benefit |
| "Exhausting work? Not for us!" | "Us" focus, unclear | "Don't Let Problems Exhaust You" | Customer-focused, clear pun |
| "We really dig plumbing" | Weak pun, "we" focus | "Don't Let Problems Drain You" | Strong pun, customer problem acknowledged |

## Description Comparisons

### Good Descriptions ✅

**Keyword + Product USP + CTA**:
- "Oil Change By ASE Certified Techs. Licensed & Insured. Call Today!" (67 chars | 0.88)
  - ✅ Starts with keyword
  - ✅ Specific credential (ASE)
  - ✅ Generic USP (Licensed & Insured)
  - ✅ Clear CTA
  - ✅ Under 90 chars

**Keyword + Generic USPs (no CTA)**:
- "Oil Change By Family-Owned Shop Serving Austin Since 1985. AAA Approved." (75 chars | 0.85)
  - ✅ Starts with keyword
  - ✅ Business type (Family-Owned)
  - ✅ History (Since 1985)
  - ✅ Credential (AAA)
  - ✅ No CTA (informational tone)

### Bad Descriptions ❌

1. **"we provide the best oil change service in austin and surrounding areas with competitive pricing"** (98 chars)
   - ❌ Lowercase
   - ❌ Exceeds 90 chars
   - ❌ "We" focus
   - ❌ Vague "best" claim
   - ❌ No specific USPs

2. **"Call us today for oil change service. We have been in business for many years and have great reviews."** (106 chars)
   - ❌ Exceeds 90 chars
   - ❌ "We" focus throughout
   - ❌ Vague "many years"
   - ❌ Vague "great reviews"
   - ❌ No Title Case

3. **"Oil Change" (11 chars)
   - ❌ Too short, wasted space
   - ❌ No USPs
   - ❌ No CTA
   - ❌ No value proposition

4. **"Get your oil changed by our expert mechanics who are ASE certified and licensed and insured and have years of experience"** (123 chars)
   - ❌ Far exceeds 90 chars
   - ❌ Run-on sentence
   - ❌ Too many "and"s
   - ❌ Unfocused (violates Element #16: Single Focus)

## Common Mistakes & Fixes

### Mistake 1: Character Limit Violations

**Bad**: "Emergency Plumbing Service Available 24 Hours A Day 7 Days A Week" (66 chars)
- ❌ Would exceed 30 char limit for headline

**Fix**: "24/7 Emergency Plumbing Service" (31 chars)
- ⚠️ Still 1 char too long

**Better Fix**: "24/7 Emergency Plumbing" (23 chars)
- ✅ Under limit, clear, uses shorthand

### Mistake 2: Lowercase Text

**Bad**: "call now for same-day service"
- ❌ All lowercase
- ❌ Unprofessional

**Fix**: "Call Now For Same-Day Service!"
- ✅ Title Case
- ✅ Added ! for emphasis

### Mistake 3: "We" Focus

**Bad**: "We Provide Expert Auto Repair"
- ❌ Starts with "We"
- ❌ Business-focused, not customer-focused

**Fix**: "Expert Auto Repair By ASE Certified Mechanics"
- ✅ Service-focused (but 45 chars - too long)

**Better Fix**: "Expert Auto Repair - ASE Certified"
- ✅ Service-focused
- ✅ 34 chars (still too long)

**Best Fix**: "Expert Auto Repair - ASE Pros"
- ✅ Service-focused
- ✅ 29 chars
- ✅ "Pros" shorter than "Certified"

### Mistake 4: Unverifiable Claims

**Bad**: "Best Auto Shop In Texas"
- ❌ Unverifiable superlative
- ❌ Will fail Google Ads policy review

**Fix**: "Rated 4.9★ By 2000+ Customers"
- ✅ Specific, verifiable
- ✅ Builds trust through social proof

### Mistake 5: No Continuity

**Bad Ad**: "Free Oil Change With Any Service!"
**Landing Page**: No mention of free oil change offer
- ❌ Violates Element #23: Continuity
- ❌ Will hurt Quality Score
- ❌ Will increase bounce rate

**Fix**: Ensure landing page prominently displays "Free Oil Change" offer OR remove from ad

## Framework Application Checklist

Use this checklist when creating RSAs:

### Tier 1 (Mandatory) ✅

- [ ] Keywords in H1-H2 (#1)
- [ ] Funnel stage matched (#2)
- [ ] All Title Case (#3)
- [ ] 2+ CTAs (#4)
- [ ] All ad claims on landing page (#23)

### Tier 2 (Preferred) ✅

- [ ] Symbols in 50%+ headlines (#11)
- [ ] Timely language (#12)
- [ ] Features + Benefits in 4+ headlines (#15)
- [ ] Single focus per headline (#16)
- [ ] 1+ problem/solution (#17)
- [ ] Pre-qualifying language (#20)

### Tier 3 (Optional) ✅

- [ ] 2 social proof headlines (#18)
- [ ] 1 pun headline (#22)
- [ ] FOMO/urgency if applicable (#14)
- [ ] Proximity for local (#10)

### Technical Validation ✅

- [ ] All headlines ≤30 chars
- [ ] All descriptions ≤90 chars
- [ ] All sentiment ≥0.8 (or ≥0.6 for problem/solution)
- [ ] All USPs verified from website
- [ ] Distribution: 3 keyword, 2 social proof, 4 generic USP, 2 CTA, 1 pun, 3 flexible
- [ ] Descriptions: 2 with CTA, 2 without
