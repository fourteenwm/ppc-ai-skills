Pricing framework for competitor analysis services across portfolios.

| Portfolio | Relationship | Pricing Approach |
|-----------|--------------|------------------|
| **Agency B** | White-label for partner/Example SaaS | Wholesale rates |

---

## Agency B Pricing (White-Label for partner)

### The Value Chain

```
Example SaaS (software) → partner (marketing) → Agency (white-label)
```

**You're not selling to auto shops directly.** You're providing value to partner that they can:
1. Use internally to improve their service
2. Pass through to Example SaaS clients (marked up)
3. Offer as a premium add-on

### Wholesale Pricing (What Agency B Charges partner)

| Tier | Competitors | Deliverable | Wholesale Price | Suggested Retail |
|------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Snapshot** | 3 | 5-7 page summary | $247-$347 | $597-$697 |
| **Full Analysis** | 5 | Client Gift (10-15 pages) | $447-$597 | $997-$1,197 |
| **Strategic Package** | 5+ | Gift + Ads Brief | $747-$897 | $1,497-$1,797 |

### Add-Ons (Wholesale)

| Add-On | Wholesale | Retail |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Additional competitors | +$75-$100 each | +$150-$200 each |
| Rush delivery (48 hours) | +$150 | +$300 |
| Quarterly monitoring | +$197/quarter | +$397/quarter |

### How to Position to partner

**Option 1: Bundled Value**
"I'm adding competitive analysis to my standard workflow. It helps me write better ads and gives you a deliverable to share with clients."
- No separate charge
- Positions you as more valuable
- May justify rate increase over time

**Option 2: A La Carte Service**
"I can provide competitive intelligence reports for your clients. Here's the wholesale pricing..."
- Separate line item
- partner marks up and resells
- Creates recurring revenue opportunity

**Option 3: Premium Tier**
"For clients who want deeper strategic support, I can do a full competitive analysis that you deliver as a premium offering."
- Upsell for high-value clients
- partner keeps the markup
- You get wholesale rate

### Time Investment

| Tier | Your Time | Hourly Equivalent |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Snapshot | 2-3 hours | $82-$173/hour |
| Full Analysis | 4-6 hours | $75-$149/hour |
| Strategic Package | 6-8 hours | $94-$150/hour |

## Agency A Pricing (Relationship Tool)

### The Context

You're an Agency A employee managing Portfolio 1, Portfolio 2, and Portfolio 3 accounts. This is not a sellable service - it's a relationship-strengthening tool.

### Primary Use: Client Gift

**Purpose:**
- Strengthen relationships with portfolio clients
- Demonstrate strategic value beyond PPC management
- Position yourself as indispensable

**Delivery:**
- Presented as "market intelligence we developed for you"
- No separate charge - part of your value
- Delivered at strategic moments (quarterly reviews, strategy sessions)

**Value to You:**
- Client retention (harder to replace someone who delivers this)
- Internal reputation (demonstrates initiative)
- Career development (strategic thinker, not just button-pusher)

### Secondary Use: New Business Support

**Purpose:**
- Support Agency A's pitches to new property management clients
- Demonstrate differentiation from other agencies

**Delivery:**
- Competitive analysis of prospect's current competitors
- Shows "we've done our homework"
- Internal cost of sale (not charged to prospect)

### Future Possibility: Agency A Service Line

If Agency A wants to productize this capability:

| Tier | Scope | Suggested Price |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Property Analysis | 1 property, 5 competitors | $1,997-$2,497 |
| Portfolio Intelligence | Full portfolio | $4,997-$7,497 |
| Quarterly Monitoring | Ongoing updates | $1,497-$1,997/quarter |

**How to propose:**
"I've developed a competitive intelligence capability that could be offered to our clients as a value-add service. Here's what it looks like and what I think we could charge..."

## Value Framing

### For Agency B/partner Conversations

**The cost of bad positioning:**
"If an auto shop spends $3,000/month on ads with the wrong message, that's $36,000/year wasted. A $500 analysis that gets the positioning right is a 72x ROI."

**The differentiation angle:**
"Most agencies just run ads. This shows clients you understand their market, not just their account."

**The retention angle:**
"Clients who get strategic insights stay longer. This isn't just a deliverable - it's a retention tool."

### For Agency A Internal Conversations

**The relationship angle:**
"Portfolio 1 sees dozens of agencies. How many of them deliver market intelligence unprompted? This is how we become the agency they can't replace."

**The efficiency angle:**
"I spend hours figuring out positioning for each account anyway. This formalizes it and creates a deliverable from work I'm already doing."

**The strategic value angle:**
"This positions Agency A as a strategic partner, not just a vendor. That's how we defend against procurement pressure."

## Handling Objections

### "We can Google our competitors ourselves"

"You could. But will you systematically analyze 5 of them, map their positioning, identify messaging gaps, and develop specific recommendations? This isn't Googling - it's strategic analysis."

### "That's expensive for a report"

"It's not a report - it's a competitive advantage. You're not paying for pages, you're paying for clarity on how to beat these businesses."

### "Can you do fewer competitors for less?"

"Sure - 3 competitors would be [lower tier price]. But honestly, 5 gives you a better picture of the market. Your call."

### "We don't need this"

"Fair enough. But when you're competing for the same customers as [competitor], knowing what they're saying and what they're NOT saying is the difference between winning and losing."
