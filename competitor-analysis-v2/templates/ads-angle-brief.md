## How to Fill This Out

### Primary Positioning Axis
From your gap identification (Phase 5), what's the SINGLE most valuable positioning opportunity?

Example: **"Trusted local expertise"** - In a market of corporate chains, client is the only family-owned option.

### Top 3 Angles
From your verified angle development (Phase 6), list the top 3 angles that:
1. Fill valuable competitive gaps
2. Are verified on client website
3. Have supporting proof

### Angles NOT to Pursue
Just as important as what TO do. List angles that:
- Competitors already own (you can't out-position them)
- Client can't prove (no website evidence)
- Are overcrowded (everyone says it, no differentiation)

### Headlines
**Character limits:**
- Google Ads headlines: 30 characters max
- Include character count for each

**Rules:**
- Must be verified from client website
- Include source citation
- Prioritize benefit over feature

### Campaign-to-Angle Map
Which angle works best for which campaign type?
- **Brand:** What makes them THEM
- **Non-Brand:** What solves the customer's problem
- **Competitor:** Why choose client over [competitor]
- **Service-Specific:** What makes THIS service special

### Compliance Watch-Outs
Flag risky claims BEFORE they become problems:
- Superlatives ("best," "#1") without substantiation
- Guarantees that aren't on the landing page
- Pricing claims that might change
- Location claims that don't match targeting

### Ad-to-Page Rules
If you're going to say it in the ad, the landing page MUST support it:
- Don't advertise "Free Quote" if the page requires credit card
- Don't advertise "24/7" if hours are limited
- Don't advertise a price if it's not on the page

### Auto Repair
- Always verify claims against the client's website data

- Common angles: Location, Amenities, Lifestyle, Value
